Events of The War

  • Year 1966 - Major Kaduna Nzeogwu led the coup which brought about the death of Alhaji Tafawa Balewa,among others.
  • Year 1966 - Yakubu Gowon led a counter coup, which also brought about the brutal death of Aguiyi Ironsi, Fajuyi, among others and the persecution of the Igbos living in the northern part of Nigeria .
  • May 1967 - Federal Military Government divided the country into 12 states from the original 4 regions. This act sparked the the desire of seccession and creation for the state of Biafra.
  • May 30, 1967 - The Biafran Government created and raised the Biafran flag
  • July 6, 1967 - The civil war began, the war to reintergrate and reunify the country Nigeria.
  • July 9, 1967 - The Biafrans led by Lt. Col Banjo retaliated by marching into the Mid Western region of Nigeria across the Niger River, passing through Benin City.
  • October 1967 - Enugu which stood as the capital of Biafra was captured by the Nigerian troops. Aba, Umuahia and Owerri served successfully as Provisional capitals.
  • September 20, 1967 - The Mid West of Nigeria was recaptured by the Nigerian Federal Troop.
  • January 28, 1968 - The Biafran Pound went public.
  • January 7, 1970 - FMG launched Operation Tail-Wind, wich was led by the 3rd Marine Commando Division and supported by the 1st and 2nd Infantry Division.
  • January 9, 1970 - Owerri, one of the Provincial Capital was captured.
  • January 10, 1970 - The Biafran Head of State, Lt. Col Ojukwu fled the Republic with his family.
  • January 11, 1970 - Uli, which habored an important Airstrip during the war, fell to the Federal Troops.
  • January 14, 1970 - The Commander of the Biafran Army, also in charge of Administrations of the Republic, surrendered to the Federal Government.
  • January 15, 1970 - The civil war officially ended, with the Federal Government proclaiming a 'No Victor No Vanquish'.
